## Understanding MultiAgent Systems
MultiAgent Systems are defined by their key characteristics: autonomy, communication, cooperation, and adaptability. Each agent operates independently while also communicating and collaborating to navigate complex environments and solve intricate problems. This decentralized approach allows for a more flexible and resilient system that can respond to changes in real-time.

Historically, the development of MAS has evolved from simple agent-based models to sophisticated systems capable of learning and adapting through machine learning and artificial intelligence. The relevance of MAS in today’s technology landscape is underscored by the increasing complexity of challenges faced by various sectors. From optimizing supply chains to managing patient care in hospitals, MAS have become integral to enhancing operational efficiency. Wooldridge's seminal work, "An Introduction to MultiAgent Systems" (2009), lays the foundation for understanding these systems and their significance in modern technology.
